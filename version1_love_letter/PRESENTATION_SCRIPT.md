## Handling Reactions

### If they CRY (good tears):
- **Do**: Hold them, give them time, wait for words
- **Say**: "I meant every equation."
- **Don't**: Panic or try to make jokes to lighten the mood

### If they LAUGH:
- **Do**: Laugh with them, it's kind of absurd and that's okay
- **Say**: "I know it's nerdy, but I'm your nerd."
- **Don't**: Get defensive‚Äîlaughter can be joy

### If they're CONFUSED:
- **Do**: Walk them through it slowly, focus on the narrative
- **Say**: "The math is just the vehicle. The message is what matters."
- **Don't**: Lecture‚Äîthis isn't a classroom

### If they're OVERWHELMED:
- **Do**: Simplify, focus on one piece at a time
- **Say**: "We can explore it together. There's no rush."
- **Don't**: Dump all the technical details at once

### If they're SKEPTICAL ("Did you really do all this?"):
- **Do**: Show them the code files, your notes, the LaTeX source
- **Say**: "Every line. Every equation. For you."
- **Don't**: Get offended‚Äîit IS a lot to believe
